\begin{align}
  \dv{}{x}c = 0

\begin{align}

### Higher-Order Derivatives

General Leibniz rule: If $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are $n$-differentiable functions, then

$$
\begin{align}
  \ndv{n}{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} \ndv{n-k}{}{x}f(x)\ndv{k}{}{x}g(x).
\end{align}
$$
