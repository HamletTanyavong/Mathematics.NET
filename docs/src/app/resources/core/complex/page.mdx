# Complex

The `Complex` type provides support for working with complex numbers in Mathematics.NET. {{ className: 'lead' }}

## Overview

Any <b>complex number</b> $z$ can be written in the form

$$
z = x + iy,
$$

where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $i$ is the imaginary unit, which has the property $i^2=-1$. The set of complex numbers is denoted by $\mathbb{C}$.[^1]

[^1]: See <i>Complex Variables and Applications</i> by James Ward Brown and Ruel V. Churchill for an introduction to Complex Analysis.

## Magnitude {{ tag: 'PROPERTY', label: 'Magnitude' }}

<Row>
  <Col>

    Get the magnitude of a complex number.

    Let $z=x+iy$, where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $i$ is the complex unit. The, the magnitude of $z$, written as $|z|$, is

    $$
    \begin{align*}
      |z| &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2}.
    \end{align*}
    $$

    <Note>
    The magnitude of a complex number is non-negative.
    </Note>

    ### Return Type

    <Properties>
      <Property name="result" type="Real">
        A real number.
      </Property>
    </Properties>

  </Col>
  <Col sticky>

    <CodeGroup title="Snippet" tag="PROPERTY" label="Magnitude">

    ```csharp
    Complex z = new(3, 4);
    z.Magnitude;
    ```

    </CodeGroup>

    ```{{ title: 'Result' }}
    5
    ```

  </Col>
</Row>

## Phase {{ tag: 'PROPERTY', label: 'Phase' }}

<Row>
  <Col>

    Get the phase of a complex number.

    Let $z=x+iy$, where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $i$ is the complex unit. The, the magnitude of $z$, written as $\arg z$, is

    $$
    \begin{align*}
      \arg z &= \atan{y}{x},
    \end{align*}
    $$

    where $-\pi<\arg z\leq\pi$.

    <Note>
    For the single-argument arctangent, $\phi=\arctan z$, the domain of the result will be different, $-\pi/2<\phi<\pi/2$.
    </Note>

    ### Return Type

    <Properties>
      <Property name="result" type="Real">
        A real number.
      </Property>
    </Properties>

  </Col>
  <Col sticky>

    <CodeGroup title="Snippet" tag="PROPERTY" label="Phase">

    ```csharp
    Complex z = new(3, 4);
    z.Phase;
    ```

    </CodeGroup>

    ```{{ title: 'Result' }}
    0.9272952180016122
    ```

  </Col>
</Row>

## Conjugate {{ tag: 'FUNCTION', label: 'Conjugate' }}

<Row>
  <Col>

    Compute the conjugate, $\overline{z}$, of a complex number.

    Let $z=x+iy$, where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $i$ is the complex unit. Then, the complex conjugate of $z$, written as $\overline{z}$, is
    $$
    \begin{align*}
      \overline{z} &= \overline{x+iy} \\
      &= x-iy.
    \end{align*}
    $$

    ### Required Parameters

      <Property name="z" type="Complex">
        A complex number.
      </Property>
    </Properties>

    ### Return Type

    <Properties>
      <Property name="result" type="Complex">
        A complex number.
      </Property>
    </Properties>

  </Col>
  <Col sticky>

    <CodeGroup title="Snippet" tag="FUNCTION" label="Conjugate">

    ```csharp
    Complex z = new(1, 2);
    Complex.Conjugate(z);
    ```

    </CodeGroup>

    ```{{ title: 'Result' }}
    (1,-2)
    ```

  </Col>
</Row>
